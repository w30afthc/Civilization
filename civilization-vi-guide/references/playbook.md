# Beginner Playbook

This is a compact coaching framework, not a rigid build order. Adapt to map, difficulty, leader, and victory goal.

## First Principles

- Civilization VI rewards tempo. Early scouting, city count, district placement, and defense usually matter more than isolated wonders.
- Cities are long-term yield engines. A mediocre early city that claims fresh water, resources, and space can outperform a delayed perfect city.
- District placement is a commitment. Lock expensive districts earlier when sensible, but do not place districts that do not support the win condition or current bottleneck.
- Eurekas and inspirations are tempo discounts. Chase the easy ones; do not distort the whole empire for a marginal boost.
- Production is the universal bottleneck. Food grows cities, but production converts plans into units, builders, districts, wonders, and projects.

## Opening Priorities

For a normal ancient-era start:

1. Scout with purpose: reveal nearby tribal villages, city-states, natural wonders, neighbors, and settlement spots.
2. Secure survival: build enough military to clear barbarians and deter rushes. Do not escort settlers with hope alone.
3. Expand early: aim for multiple cities before over-investing in wonders. Fresh water, resources, and defensible positions often beat perfect adjacency.
4. Improve key tiles: prioritize tiles that solve immediate bottlenecks, trigger eurekas/inspirations, or unlock luxuries/strategic resources.
5. Choose a first victory direction: science, culture, domination, religion, diplomacy, or flexible economy.

## City Placement Heuristics

Prefer:

- Fresh water or strong housing plan.
- At least one or two workable high-yield tiles immediately.
- A luxury or strategic resource claim.
- Good district triangles: campus near mountains/reefs/geothermal; holy site near mountains/natural wonders/woods; commercial hub on rivers; harbor on coast; industrial zones near aqueduct/dam/canal clusters in Gathering Storm.
- Defensible terrain and reasonable distance from loyalty pressure.

Avoid:

- Delaying settlement too long for a "perfect" spot.
- Settling every city at maximum distance if the empire becomes hard to defend.
- Building a district only because it has high adjacency when it does not support the plan.

## District Priorities

- **Science victory**: campuses, industrial zones, commercial hubs/harbors, spaceport cities with strong production.
- **Culture victory**: theater squares, holy sites or faith economy if using naturalists/rock bands, commercial hubs/harbors, wonders only when they support tourism or great works.
- **Domination victory**: encampments when useful, campuses for tech timing, commercial hubs/harbors for upgrade gold, industrial zones for production.
- **Religious victory**: holy sites early, faith economy, shrines/temples, apostles with useful promotions, diplomacy to avoid dying while spreading.
- **Diplomatic victory**: economy, city-state envoys, wonders and emergencies/competitions where relevant, culture for key civics.

## First 100 Turns Checklist

Use this as a diagnostic list:

- Have I met nearby city-states and neighbors?
- Are barbarians under control?
- Do I have enough cities for the map and difficulty?
- What is my bottleneck: production, science, culture, gold, faith, amenities, housing, loyalty, or military?
- Are my policy cards aligned with what I am producing now?
- Am I triggering easy eurekas/inspirations?
- Do I have trade routes online or planned?
- Am I overbuilding wonders instead of settlers, builders, units, or districts?
- Is there a clear victory path or a near-term pivot?

## Common Beginner Mistakes

- Building too few cities.
- Ignoring barbarians until a camp snowballs.
- Chasing every wonder.
- Working low-yield tiles because citizens are unmanaged.
- Building every district everywhere.
- Forgetting policy card swaps.
- Neglecting culture while pursuing science.
- Neglecting economy while pursuing war.
- Starting wars without siege, upgrade gold, or strategic resources.
- Waiting too long to convert a general advantage into a victory condition.

## Victory Route Mini-Plans

### Science

Build enough cities, prioritize campuses and production, keep culture adequate for governments/policies, secure strategic resources, and designate high-production spaceport cities. Late game is about projects, power, engineers/scientists, and production modifiers.

### Culture

Accumulate tourism sources: great works, wonders, national parks, seaside resorts, improvements, rock bands, and policy multipliers. Culture defends against others' tourism and unlocks key civics. Faith is often useful even when not pursuing religion.

### Domination

Hit timing windows: archers, horsemen/swordsmen, crossbows, bombards, cuirassiers, artillery, bombers, etc. Bring enough siege and economy for upgrades. Capture productive cities, repair, stabilize loyalty, then roll forward.

### Religion

Found early, enhance beliefs that fit the map, produce apostles/missionaries efficiently, defend theological combat, and convert civs decisively rather than spreading thinly everywhere.

### Diplomacy

Track diplomatic victory points, emergencies, competitions, city-state suzerainty, aid requests, wonders, and late civics/techs. Build a flexible economy and avoid diplomatic penalties that undermine votes.
